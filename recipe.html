Healthy Burger

Intro

  • Time: 25 min
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Bread 2
Lettuce 1 slice
Onion 1 slice
Tomatoe 1 slice
Cheese 1 slice
Chicken patties 1

Step 1: Add your ground chicken and the rest of your burger ingredients to a large bowl.
Step 2: Mix well we want everything combined.
Step 3: Shape your chicken burger patties. Try to shape them a little larger than your buns as they will shrink somewhat as they cook.
Step 4: If grilling, it’s a good idea to chill your patties or pop in the freezer for 30-60 minutes before grilling.
Step 5: Grill 5-6 minutes per side, just until a meat thermometer inserted in the center reaches 165 degrees F.
Step 6: Serve.

French Fries

Intro

  • Time: 30 min
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Patatoes 2 large russet
Oil 3 cups
Salt 2 pinches
Pepper 3 pinches

Step 1: Stir together warm water and sugar in a medium bowl. Soak potatoes in water mixture for 15 minutes. Remove potatoes from water and dry thoroughly on paper towels.
Step 2: Heat oil in a deep-fryer to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
Step 3: Cook potatoes in hot oil until golden, 5 to 6 minutes. Drain on paper towels and season with salt.

Chocolate Ice Cream

Intro

  • Time: 10 min
  • Serves: 10
Ingredients Quantity
Whole milk 1/2 cup
Cocoa powder 1/2 cup
Sugar 1 cup
Heavy cream 2 cups
Vanilla Extract 1 teaspoon

Step 1: Gather the ingredients.
Step 2: Whisk together the milk, cocoa powder, and sugar. Whisk until the sugar and cocoa completely dissolve.
Step 3: In a large bowl, beat the heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks. Fold the whipped cream along with the vanilla extract into the cocoa powder mixture.
Step 4: Refrigerate the mixture for at least 30 minutes so it is completely cold. This will help it freeze faster, improve the texture, and allow the cocoa powder to become fully hydrated by the milk and cream.
Step 5: Give the ice cream base one more gentle stir and freeze according to the directions of your ice cream maker—about 1 1/2 to 2 hours.
Step 6: Store your ice cream in an airtight plastic container in the back of the freezer. This will help preserve the flavor and texture of the finished ice cream. You can serve the chocolate ice cream right from the ice cream maker, but it will have the texture of soft serve. Freezing for an hour before serving will create a firmer texture.

Source: Chocolate Ice Cream
